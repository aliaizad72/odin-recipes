Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi Lemak is Malaysia's national dish. Usually eaten during breakfast or dinner (or lunch or late night ...), Nasi Lemak in its most basic form is coconut rice paired with sambal (chilli paste), peanuts and anchovies. Slices of cucumber are also included on the side, along with half of boiled egg.

Though not portrayed in the image, Nasi Lemak is also commonly eaten with protein such as chicken (usually fried), more eggs, squids, or cow's lungs (boiled or fried).

Ingredients

Coconut milk steamed rice

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 3 screwpine leaves aka pandan leaves (tie in knots)
  • Salt to taste
  • 5.6oz of coconut milk
  • Water

Tamarind juice

  • 1 cup of water
  • Tamarind pulp

Sambal

  • 1/2 of red onion
  • 1 cup of anchovies
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 4 shallots
  • 10 dried chillies
  • 1 teaspoon of belacan (prawn paste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar

Other ingredients

  • 2 hard boiled eggs (cut in half)
  • Your choice of protein
  • Slices of cucumber

Steps

  1. To cook the rice, wash and drain as per usual. In addition to the water, add in coconut milk and salt before cooking. Lastly, add in pandan leaves before leaving it to cook.
  2. Fry the anchovies until they turn light brown
  3. Slice the red onions into rings. Smash the belacan with shallots, garlic and deseeded dried chillies with a mortar and pestle or a blender.
  4. Soak tamarind juice in water for 15 minutes. Squeeze the tamarind pulp to extract the flavor into the water. Drain the pulp and keep the tamarind juice.
  5. Fry the sambal until fragrant. Add in onion rings and ikan bilis. Stir well. Add tamarind juice, salt and sugar. Simmer on low heat until the gravy thickens. Set aside.
  6. Cook your protein however you like. Dish up the cooked coconut milk rice and pour some sambal on top of it. Put slices of cucumber and eggs alongside them. Lastly, put the protein of choice in as well.
  7. Enjoy!
