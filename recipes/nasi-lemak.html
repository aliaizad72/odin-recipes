Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi Lemak is Malaysia's national dish. Usually eaten during breakfast or dinner (or lunch or late night ...), Nasi Lemak in its most basic form is coconut rice paired with sambal (chilli paste), peanuts and anchovies. Slices of cucumber are also included on the side, along with half of boiled egg.

Though not portrayed in the image, Nasi Lemak is also commonly eaten with protein such as chicken (usually fried), more eggs, squids, or cow's lungs (boiled or fried).

Ingredients

Coconut milk steamed rice

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 3 screwpine leaves aka pandan leaves (tie in knots)
  • Salt to taste
  • 5.6oz of coconut milk
  • Water

Tamarind juice

  • 1 cup of water
  • Tamarind pulp

Sambal

  • 1/2 of red onion
  • 1 cup of anchovies
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 4 shallots
  • 10 dried chillies
  • 1 teaspoon of belacan (prawn paste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar

Other ingredients

  • 2 hard boiled eggs (cut in half)
  • Your choice of protein
  • Slices of cucumber
